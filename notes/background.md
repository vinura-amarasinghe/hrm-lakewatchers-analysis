# Background Notes

Working notes on the analysis context. Not part of the published report —
this is my reference material.

## Parameters of interest

### Temperature (water)
- Why it matters: drives stratification, dissolved oxygen solubility, and
  rate of biological processes. Warmer water holds less DO. Warming trends
  often correlate with cyanobacteria bloom risk.
- Healthy range: depends on lake type and season.
- Guideline: CCME — no single threshold; site-specific.

### Dissolved oxygen
- Why it matters: critical for fish and invertebrates.
- CCME guideline for aquatic life (cold-water early life stages): ≥ 9.5 mg/L.
- CCME guideline for aquatic life (warm-water other life stages): ≥ 5.5 mg/L.

### Total phosphorus
- Why it matters: primary driver of eutrophication in freshwater systems.
- CCME trigger ranges:
  - Ultra-oligotrophic: < 4 µg/L
  - Oligotrophic: 4–10 µg/L
  - Mesotrophic: 10–20 µg/L
  - Meso-eutrophic: 20–35 µg/L
  - Eutrophic: 35–100 µg/L
  - Hyper-eutrophic: > 100 µg/L

### Chlorophyll-a
- Why it matters: proxy for algal biomass and trophic status.
- Trophic-status indicator alongside total phosphorus.

### E. coli (if available)
- Why it matters: indicator of fecal contamination, recreational water
  safety.
- Health Canada Guidelines for Recreational Water Quality (2023):
  Beach Action Value of 235 CFU/100 mL for a single sample.
